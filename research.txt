Here are strong, actionable ideas for a customer-facing airline mobile (and web) app. These draw from current trends in 2025–2026, top-performing airline apps (like Qatar Airways' award-winning app, United, Delta, and Lufthansa), and common traveler pain points such as disruptions, navigation stress, lost baggage anxiety, lack of personalization, and sustainability concerns.

Key Trends & Insights from Research
Airline apps have evolved from basic booking/check-in tools into full-journey companions. Standout features include:

AI personalization and conversational assistants — Qatar Airways' Sama (AI "Cabin Crew") handles natural voice/text queries for itineraries, destinations, prices, and meal recommendations. Lufthansa's Holly offers personalized trip planning and packing lists. Wizz Air's Amelia provides proactive disruption support.
1
Real-time visibility and control — United excels with package-style bag tracking, connection tools (countdowns, directions, ConnectionSaver that holds planes), Virtual Gate boarding progress, and personalized layover tips. Delta uses Live Activities for lock-screen updates on boarding, seats, and baggage carousels.
2
Seamless & immersive experiences — Biometrics, digital wallets, flexible calendars (Delta), and early AR uses (e.g., KLM's bag size checker via camera overlay).
Sustainability and loyalty — Growing demand for carbon tracking, eco-options, and meaningful rewards.
Pain points apps should solve — Flight delays/cancellations (biggest frustration), baggage issues, confusing airport navigation (especially connections/hubs), overwhelming or generic experiences, and family/group coordination.
Your app should feel calming, proactive, trustworthy, and delightful — using clean blues/greens, generous whitespace, micro-animations (e.g., smooth boarding pass reveal), and a prominent AI assistant. Prioritize one-tap actions, offline support, accessibility (voice, large text, high contrast), and a modular dashboard.

Product Concepts You Can Build Into a Portfolio Project
Here are four focused, innovative concepts. Pick one as your main project or combine elements (e.g., a core app with standout modules). Each can be prototyped in Figma with user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity screens.

1. VistaCompanion: AI-Powered Personal Journey Orchestrator (Most Recommended for Differentiation)
An intelligent, proactive "travel brain" that feels like having a personal cabin crew + concierge in your pocket.

Core Value: Turns reactive travel into effortless, personalized experiences. Addresses disruptions, planning fatigue, and lack of tailoring.

Key Features:

Conversational AI assistant (voice/text, inspired by Sama/Amelia) that builds/optimizes itineraries, suggests alternatives during delays, recommends meals/seats based on preferences/history/diet, and generates packing lists (weather, activities, duration).
Proactive disruption handler: Real-time alerts with one-tap rebooking, compensation claims, hotel vouchers, or connection protection.
Personalized dashboard: Journey timeline with smart cards (next action, weather at destination, wellness tips like jet lag mitigation, carbon footprint).
Loyalty integration: Visible miles/points with contextual "burn" suggestions (e.g., upgrade prompt at checkout).
UX Highlights to Showcase:

Natural language home screen ("Book a relaxing beach trip next month under $800").
Emotional design: Calming animations during delays, progress bars for connections.
Multi-passenger mode for families.
This is highly portfolio-friendly — you can mock AI chat flows, A/B test recommendation cards, and show before/after disruption scenarios.

2. SkyFlow: Seamless Airport & Real-Time Visibility Hub
Focuses on the highest-stress parts: getting to/from the airport and moving through it.

Core Value: Reduces anxiety in large hubs with predictive, visual tools (builds on United's strengths and emerging AR).

Key Features:

AR indoor navigation: Point camera for overlaid directions to gates, lounges, restrooms, food (with live wait times/queues), and baggage claim. Include accessibility routes.
Advanced baggage tracking: Package-delivery-style map (like United) + AirTag-style sharing with the airline for lost bags. Predictive carousel arrival.
Pre-arrival prep: Digital bag sizer (AR overlay like KLM), document checker, and "airport simulator" for your specific terminal.
Connection optimizer: Turn-by-turn walking times, lounge suggestions, and automated alerts if you're running late.
UX Highlights: Interactive airport maps, live status widgets/Live Activities, and a "stress meter" that surfaces helpful tips. Great for demonstrating spatial UI and motion design.

3. HarmonyFly: Group & Family Travel Coordinator
Many apps handle solo travelers well but struggle with groups — this fills the gap.

Core Value: Makes traveling with others (families, friends, sports teams) seamless and less chaotic.

Key Features:

Shared itineraries with permissions (one person books, everyone sees real-time updates and can vote on extras).
Group seat selection on map with visual clustering.
Family modes: Kid-friendly activity suggestions during layovers, synchronized notifications, child profiles (meal prefs, seating needs).
Split payments, shared loyalty earning, and group check-in/boarding pass distribution.
UX Highlights: Collaborative elements (real-time sync indicators), simple sharing flows, and customizable views (parent vs. child mode). Perfect for showing user research with personas and complex user flows.

4. EcoVista: Sustainable & Personalized Booking Experience (Great Add-On or Niche Project)
Integrate sustainability without making it preachy.

Core Value: Helps eco-conscious travelers make better choices and feel good about them, while tying into loyalty.

Key Features:

Carbon calculator during search with "greenest" sorting/filter (trains/connections where viable, efficient aircraft).
Visual impact dashboard: "This trip = X trees planted equivalent" with easy offset options that earn bonus miles.
Personalized eco-recommendations blended with preferences (e.g., "This flight has 25% lower emissions and matches your window seat preference").
Challenges/badges for loyalty (e.g., "Low-carbon traveler" tier).
UX Highlights: Beautiful data visualizations, comparison sliders for flight options, and positive reinforcement animations.

How to Turn These Into a Strong Job Application Project
Research Phase: Create 3–4 personas (e.g., busy business traveler, family of 4, eco-conscious millennial, elderly solo traveler). Map current pain-point journeys vs. your improved flows.
Competitive Analysis: Review Qatar, United, Delta, Emirates apps (screenshots or actual use). Note what's missing or could be better.
Design Deliverables:
Low/mid/high-fidelity wireframes and prototypes (focus on 5–8 key screens + flows).
Micro-interactions (boarding pass animation, AI response typing, AR demo).
Design system (components, icons, typography) aligned with airline branding — professional yet approachable.
Accessibility audit and dark mode.
Testing & Validation: Plan or simulate usability tests. Measure metrics like task completion time for booking or rebooking.
Presentation: In your portfolio/case study, lead with the problem (backed by trends), show research, then your solution with visuals. End with impact (e.g., "Reduces disruption stress by providing control").
Bonus Tips:

Make the AI feel human and helpful, not robotic — include safeguards like "escalate to human" easily.
Ensure privacy/transparency around data use for personalization.
Consider web progressive app (PWA) elements for broader access.
For inspiration on visuals: Search Dribbble/Behance for "airline app UI" — lots of modern card-based, immersive designs with destination photography and clean timelines.
These ideas position you as forward-thinking while being grounded in real user needs and what leading airlines are already doing successfully. They're scalable — you can start with one core flow (e.g., booking + AI assistant) and expand.
